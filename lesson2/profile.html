Apple
Apple Inc., founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne, began with the Apple I and gained success with the Apple II. The Macintosh in 1984 introduced a graphical user interface, revolutionizing personal computing. After struggles in the 1990s, Jobs returned in 1997, leading to iconic innovations like the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad. Today, Apple is a global tech leader known for design and innovation.
Samsung
Samsung, founded in 1938 in South Korea by Lee Byung-chul, started as a trading company. It entered electronics in the 1960s, producing TVs, semiconductors, and home appliances. By the 2000s, Samsung became a leader in mobile technology, with its Galaxy smartphone series rivaling Apple's iPhone. Today, Samsung is a global powerhouse in electronics, semiconductors, and appliances, driving innovation in consumer tech

Cars

"Cars" (2006) is an animated film produced by Pixar Animation Studios and released by Walt Disney Pictures. The story follows Lightning McQueen, a race car who ends up stranded in the small, forgotten town of Radiator Springs after a mishap on his way to a big race. While trying to find his way back to the competition, McQueen forms unexpected friendships with the town's quirky residents, including Mater, a rusty tow truck, and Sally, a Porsche with a passion for the town's history. The film explores themes of friendship, community, and the value of slowing down to enjoy life. It's a heartwarming adventure with a mix of humor, stunning animation, and a focus on personal growth. The success of Cars led to two sequels, Cars 2 (2011) and Cars 3 (2017).
