Ferrari, founded by Enzo Ferrari in 1939, began as a racing division of Alfa Romeo. Its first car, the 125 S, debuted in 1947. Renowned for motorsport dominance, Ferrari is the most successful Formula 1 team, with iconic drivers like Michael Schumacher. Blending racing innovation with luxury, Ferrari's legendary cars include the 250 GTO, Testarossa, and modern hybrids like the LaFerrari

Engine & Performance:
The F40 is powered by a 2.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine,
producing 471 horsepower and 426 lb-ft of torque.

It accelerates from 0 to 60 mph in just 3.8 seconds, with a top speed of over 200 mph (322 km/h)
making it one of the fastest production cars of its time.

The engine is mounted in a rear-mid position, and the car is equipped with a 5-speed manual transmission. The F40’s performance is raw and unassisted, with no traction control or ABS, mark which makes it a true driver's car, requiring skill and focus to handle.
The Ferrari F40's engine sound is a symphony of raw power and precision engineering, a true testament to the passion and performance of the brand.



Price

  • Low-Mileage, Pristine Condition: Price Range: $1,400,000 – $2,000,000+ These are the top-tier cars, often with low miles (under 10,000 miles), fully documented history, and in concours-level condition. Cars with particularly low mileage or exceptional provenance (e.g., celebrity ownership) can sometimes command even higher prices.

  • Well-Preserved, High-Mileage Examples: Price Range: $900,000 – $1,400,000 These cars typically have a few more miles on them, but still present in excellent mechanical and aesthetic condition. They may show signs of usage but are still considered highly collectible.

  • Good Condition with Higher Mileage/Modifications: Price Range: $600,000 – $900,000 F40s in this range have typically been driven more and may show some wear. Some may have had modifications or restoration work done, which can influence the price depending on the nature of the changes.

  • Project Cars/Restoration Candidates: Price Range: $400,000 – $600,000 These cars might need significant work, either in terms of mechanical repairs or cosmetic restoration. While they are still valuable as part of the F40 lineage, they require time and investment to bring them back to their prime.
